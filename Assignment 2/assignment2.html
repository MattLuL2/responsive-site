Agriculture and Soil

Agriculture is one of the key ingredients in reducing the number of people who go hungry.
"In many parts of the world, intensification and expansion of agriculture has degraded soils and ecosystems, depleted water sources and reduced biodiversity. As a result, farmers often have no choice but to search for new land to work or to use soil as a substrate with massive synthetic inputs.3"

Drylands and Land Degradation

Drylands are defined as having one or several of the following characteristics:

  1. "scarcity of water, but not necessarily low rainfall."                                                
  2. "high levels of potential water loss."                                                                     
  3. "the potential loss of water is significantly greater than the mean precipitation."      
  4. "high evapotranspiration rates and high uncertainty of precipitation"                       
  5. "Precipitation in drylands can be high.4"

Effective Protected Areas

Areas that are protected or conserved safeguard nature and cultural resources and further sustainable growth.
"Protected areas are at the core of efforts towards conserving nature and the services it provides us - food, clean water supply, medicines and protection from the impacts of natural disasters. Their role in helping mitigate and adapt to climate change is also increasingly recognised; it has been estimated that the global network of protected areas stores at least 15% of terrestrial carbon.5"
